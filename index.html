Leonardo da Vinci

The Renaissance Genius


Here's a timeline of Leonardo da Vinci's life and some of his most notable works:

  • 1452 - Born in Vinci, a region of Florence, Italy

  • c.Mid-1460's - Became a studio boy for Verrocchio, a leading Florentine painter and sculptor

  • 1472 - Qualified as a master in the Guild of Saint Luke, the guild of artists and doctors of medicine

  • 1972-75 - Painted The Baptism of Christ with Verrocchio

  • 1478 - Received his first independent commission to paint an altarpiece for the Chapel of St. Bernard in the Palazzo Vecchio

  • c.1483-86 - Painted The Virgin of the Rocks

  • c.1490 - Pen-and-ink drawing of Vitruvian Man

  • 1495-98 - Painted The Last Supper

  • c.1500 - Painted Salvator Mundi

  • 1502 - Entered the service of Cesare Borgia, the son of Pope Alexander VI, acting as a military architect and engineer and travelling throughout Italy with his patron

  • 1503 - Returned to Florence where he rejoined the Guild of Saint Luke, and began his portrait of Lisa del Giocondo, the model for the Mona Lisa

  • 1503 - Started designing and painting The Battle of Anghiari

  • 1512 - Equestrian monument prevented from being completed due to an invasion of a confederation of Swiss, Spanish and Venetian forces, which drove the French from Milan

  • 1519 - Dies at the age of 67.
